Instruction Chapter 1

Chapter 1

After following the cat down the street, you're brought back to an old house at the end of the street, just bordering the forest. The house is surrounded by a swarm of cats, each of various forms, sizes, and colours, and staring at you with wide eyes. Just when you turn to go back, you spot small piles of junk nearby. On closer inspection, you notice a raggedy notebook that looks somewhat familiar? You realize it belonged to the previous detective! But due to some missing pages, any discernable information doesn't make much sense. Regardless, this must be around where he and the missing people have disappeared. Determined with a new lead, you approach the front of the house, hoping one last time to find someone with information, but before you can knock, the door opens:

You think back to the small cat from earlier. It seemed more used to humans than the others, could it have been hers? But she said 'two years ago', it shouldn't still be alive. With all these cats around, how does she even notice one’s missing? Whatever it is, she's also managed to sense your presence. Before dwelling on it more you push those thoughts aside. Remember, prioritize the case, and she doesn't seem to know of anything useful. You're about to end the conversation when suddenly, the old lady speaks:

Before you can protest, she's already guiding you inside. Despite your hesitance, you comply, not only to discover a new lead, but because she doesn't seem the type to take “no” for an answer.

Part 2

The interior is a little cluttered and populated with cats. A small kitchen and a glass door leading to the backyard are located down the corridor. You see that it has slightly creaked open, which makes you feel chillier as you approach it. To the side of the kitchen entrance is the freshly made tea atop the kitchen counter and stacked with shelves of bottles, each with labels you can't quite read. A little wooden table sits just off-centre from the kitchen's entrance, and the old lady motions for you to join her:

The longer you stay, the less likely you're getting any new leads. While stating she's not seen anyone new, all you've managed to learn is about her cat. He'd often slip away into small spaces, or the distinctive orange spots against his dark fur made him stand out in addition to having bi-coloured eyes; you get the sense that they're similar to your own. And whenever she isn't reminiscing about him, her attention drifts to you instead of your questions. Just as you try to steer the conversation back, she shifts again, almost absently: It's not uncommon for detectives to work alone, but nobody else has bothered to come. You're almost about to reply. But you're interrupted when the tea is ready and expectantly placed in front of you.
