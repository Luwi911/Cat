Chapter 1

You are a detective, fresh and determined to make a change in your community, however, you haven't exactly had the chance to do so. Being new, you're subject to simple tasks like paper work and fetching documents for your senior colleagues. One day, you're slaving away at your desk when you overhear the Captain trying to recruit another detective for an old three-missing-persons case. Initially it was only two people, but word has it the case's previous detective has also gone missing. It's been two years since then, and with the public getting restless, the captain feels pressure in looking for someone else to take over. The hesitance of everyone else leads you to spring at the opportunity:

Despite the Captain's initial hesitance, the potential to shed the public pressure wins him over. Even if cold cases are notorious for being difficult, your whole reason for joining this agency was to help the community, and lately you've been getting tired of the constant paperwork. A challenge is just what you need in order to make a proper impact.

Here is the case file containing profiles on the two missing people, and the previous detective. Most of the existing evidence has long expired, leaving the only major clue as their last known location; a rundown neighbourhood on the edge of town, bordering a forest. Looks like you have your work cut out for you, and in haste, you set out for any potential leads.

The trees along the sides get denser and the end of the neighbourhood is a forest sectioned off by a creepy, short stone wall. It's clear how this part marks the edge of town when you arrive. The neighbourhood is quiet, so quiet that you'd thought it to be abandoned if not for the amount of cats nearby. Despite the eeriness, you walk down the road, planning to survey the area for any leads. But along the way, you get the sense that you're being followed… You turn around to see an orange cat close behind. It seems different from the ones in the street, almost familiar? After being spotted, the cat sprints off in a direction ahead then stops to look back, it seems it's waiting for you to follow.
