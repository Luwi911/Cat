Chapter 2

Your eyes open slowly to allow your vision to readjust. The grogginess doesn't let you register straight away, but you slowly wake up in what seems to be a small compartment of the house when you notice a cat looming over you. You jolt up but notice something strange. A small grey cat was peering down at you with concerned eyes.

You stumble back at the cat's words. Did you seriously understand that? What's going on? Where are you? All these questions are too overwhelming. Despite the headache, you get the impression of a strange arrangement of items in the room. Not only do you feel lighter and slightly warm, but is everything larger than necessary? Then you take notice of a dusty mirror and what's reflected back at you is not what you expected; you've been transformed into a cat. In a panic, questions spew out one after another:

You take the time to assess who you're speaking with. A small grey cat, with a fluffy exterior. Her small, rounded stature gives a youthful impression, and you wonder what she may have looked like before ending up here. You're shocked to learn her name is Charlie, and that every cat you saw were once humans who lived in these parts. It's unclear how long they've been this way, they've largely converted to their new lives, and it's slowly taking effect for the cat before you too. Besides your body, forgetting your speech will be the first to go, and soon enough your mentality before completing the transformation. Since the small cat was transformed more recently, around 6 months, you're still able to understand each other but every now and then you catch a couple meows infecting her speech.

Determined to stop this, you try to search for clues as to how the old lady has turned you all into cats. Along the wall is a clear cupboard filled with bottles you recognize from the kitchen.You couldn't read their labels before, but you realise this is what was slipped into your tea. You spot what looks like a bookshelf, but from the floor their titles are impossible to make out. You leap onto the nearby desk for a better angle, only to find rows of books marked with a single letter. The cat's warning only heightens your curiosity. If the books follow a pattern, then a passcode has to be hidden nearby. You're about to climb down when you notice the desk drawer slightly open; something pale catches the light from the shadows—a scrap of paper lying flat on the wood, completely hidden if you weren't slim enough, slim enough to get closer.
