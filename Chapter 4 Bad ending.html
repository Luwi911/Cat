Chapter 4

Just as you're about to come to a boiling point, the witch takes her eyes off you for a moment, and you seize the opportunity to tip the cauldron, spilling the resurrection potion. The effort needed for tipping it over was more than you realized and you're left with a bruised side. While you're largely unscathed, there are others who seemed to suffer from much worse; most notably it seems the boiling potion left scathing marks along their bodies. Suddenly, you all feel a shivering feeling so intensely you pass out.

You regain consciousness and feel the damp grass on your skin. Wait, skin. Your eyes shoot open and you see mist pouring out of the pot, but then see human hands on the grass. You're human again. Even while you're surrounded by cloud of mist, you can still make out to shape of other human silhouettes, but a then sudden shriek disrupts your relief :

A sudden cackle interrupts your sentence and it seems the witch finally lost it, but she gets more maniacal by the second

After the mist thins, it unveils an alarming sight. Patches of fur cling to arms and legs, pointy ears twitch involuntarily, and tails flick rapidly in reaction to stress. And worse, the look in everyone's eyes. Something's wrong. Sudden movements remain on edge, too reactive, too animal. You realize that your interference didn't completely reverse the effects, it fused it with your human forms.

Half a year has passed since your first case and you were forced to return to your daily lives. The remaining residents of the witch's neighbourhood have grown accustomed to their new forms, but the four of you are permanently changing how you navigate the world in order to reintegrate back to your old lives. The adverse effects have a long-term impact on your physical and emotional health, because despite having watched her melt into a steaming pile of goo, you're more haunted by the memory of witch's screams of agony and grief. Although you're 'human' again, there'll forever be a constant reminder of your brash actions lingering with you.
