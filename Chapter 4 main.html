You quietly read the reversal spell, and for probably the first time in months, the barrier breaks free but it was anything but silent. The Witch quickly takes notice of the ruckus and shrieks in alarm. Even with the bottles in her hands, the witch attempts to make lunges at you. One of the plus sides of being a cat now is your heightened skills of agility to manage avoiding her grasp. In a last ditch effort, a lever is slammed down to manually force the bookshelf door closed, but it proves futile when all of you made it outside in time. You appear to be safe for a while, because what was meant to stop you ended up becoming an obstacle for the witch herself, for it seems the bookshelf's mechanics require a cooldown before being opened again. Finally you can all take a breath and recuperate your plan; with Charlie having been in solitary confinement and Remy having been trapped behind the secret bookshelf room, none of you have proper familiarity with this place. And so, you resume investigating the house except not only for victims, but for an antidote and an escape.

While dashing into a hallway; it's cold and chilly but you can already hear the bookshelf being clawed back open. You spot more paintings of Sesame on the walls; a stark contrast to the front of the house where you followed the witch through. You figure that finding your way back to the kitchen will be your best chance of escaping, except neither of you know where to go. You take the one clear route through the blockage of cats, but a large cat refuses to move. The patterns on its body make you realize it's the same cat you encountered upon your arrival to this neighbourhood, but you feel as if you've seen it somewhere else, except now is not the time for reminiscing.

The orange cat leads you all to the forest where you briefly stop to catch your breath. There’s no sign of the witch now and the amount of cats blocking the way may keep her busy for a while. This is the moment you properly look at the larger cat who saved your lives; it's bulkier in size and seems older than the others. Its bright coat features specs of brown, white and orange along its back and a brown patch around its right eye. It slowly blinks, and you realize why it looks so familiar; it's the previous detective. He leads you all to a magic circle, just like the torn piece of paper described. You try to converse with the detective about how this all happened and where he’s been, but you're met with a quiet, knowing silence.
