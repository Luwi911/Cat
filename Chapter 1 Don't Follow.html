Chapter 1

You decide to ignore the cat, knowing you don't have time to risk getting side tracked. You attempt to look for leads by knocking on the doors but for some reason not ones answered? The further you walk, the number of cats increases, but these ones don't acknowledge your presence the same as the cat from earlier. They seem to cluster near a house at the end of the neighbourhood. It's old, and directly borders the forest overall producing an offputting atmosphere, but you haven't found a single person home so far. Maybe all the cats gathered here mean something? Curious, you approach the door, hoping you'll finally find someone with information, but before you can knock, the door opens:

You think back to the small cat from earlier, it seemed more used to humans than the others, could it have been hers? But she said 'two years ago'. If it's truly been missing it shouldn't still be alive. And with all these other cats, I wonder how she acquired so many? Before dwelling on it more you push those thoughts aside. Remember, prioritize the case, and she doesn't seem to know of anything useful. You're about to end the conversation when suddenly, the old lady speaks:

Before you can protest, she's already guiding you inside. You comply, not only to discover a new lead, but because she doesn't seem the type to take “no” for an answer.

Part 2

The interior is a little cluttered and populated with cats. A small kitchen and a glass door leading to the backyard are located down the corridor. You see that it has slightly creaked open, which makes you feel chillier as you approach it. To the side of the kitchen entrance is the freshly made tea atop the kitchen counter and stacked with shelves of bottles, each with labels you can't quite read. A little wooden table sits just off-centre from the kitchen's entrance, and the old lady motions for you to join her:

The longer you stay, the less likely you're getting any new leads. While stating she's not seen anyone new, all you've managed to learn is about her cat. He'd often slip away into small spaces, or the distinctive orange spots against his dark fur made him stand out in addition to having bi-coloured eyes; you get the sense that they're similar to your own. And whenever she isn't reminiscing about him, her attention drifts to you instead of your questions. Just as you try to steer the conversation back, she shifts again, almost absently: It's not uncommon for detectives to work alone, but nobody else has bothered to come. You're almost about to reply.
